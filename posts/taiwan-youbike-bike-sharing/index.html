YouBike - An amazing bike-sharing system in Taiwan

September 30, 2020 5 minutes read
taiwan • taipei

YouBike (or Ubike) is my first choice of transportation when it comes to move around the city of Taipei. I largely prefer it over taking the subway, bus or taxi. It is affordable, well maintained, comfortable, at waking distance reach from anywhere, and the cities has built great biking paths in the major cities.

The number of YouBike rides keeps increasing steadily since it was launched in 2012. There was 170 millions rides since it was introduced in 2012, and last month it reached ~3 millions rides in Taipei:

A high quality infrastructure

YouBikes are everywhere in Taipei and New Taipei City . As for October 2020, over 42000 YouBikes are deployed, with over 1000 stations. Stations get added every few weeks.

Since the first time I arrive in Taipei (2016), I am pleasantely suprised that I rarely got a broken bike. Giant is actually the (local) company that provides them. The bikes feels durable, lightweight, and really well maintained. Each bike is bought by the city for around 9200 TWD (~USD 300), and comes with a 7 years maintenance.

To make it really convenient, the city has organized each station from 200 to 600 meters from each others. What make you within 5 to 10 minutes walk from wherever you are in the city.

Data driven decisions

I’ve noticed that station are also rarely empty. From time to time, I’ve spotted some employees “reloading” or “deloading” stations that are full or empty. Who are they? Where do they take those bikes? I’ve discussed with Alex Garcia and Tim Cho, two urbanism specialists of the city of Taipei.

1 or 2 employees are reponsible for a given area to “unload” or “refill” the stations. But how do they decide if some station should be “refilled” or not? Is a single empty station enough to make an employee move to the station? Not necessarly. Most of the time, an area of few stations being almost empty will make it worthwile to move. This is a “cluster analysis”. Alex mentioned me they also have “re-balancing” trucks equiped with an application with a smart algorithm telling them where to unload thes stations that are full, and reload empty ones.

To make their work easier, historical data are used to predict the flow-in & flow out for each station. They know the patterns on which stations will be empty and which one will need a refill. Those stations often have a “buffer” of bikes nearby locked with together in bulk. When the station is about to get empty, the employee responsible for this area will drive thre and refill the empty station with the buffer of bikes alread present.

To make the decisions to add new stations, Tim explained they use GIS spatial analysis, to realize uncovered area (population density, schools, presence of metro station, POIs, etc…) to make the decision to add or not a new YouBike station.

Open data

Taipei city (and Taiwan in general) makes an amazing job at opening data. It provides real time data showing each Youbike station status. Any developer can offer their own application to help users to find bikes avaibility. This is how I’ve calculated the number of YouBikes in Taipei.

Jakub told me he used this open data to help him stop being late at class. He depended of the YouBike to go to NTUST but ended up always late because his nearest station was empty. He started recording the the patterns/waves of bikes coming and leaving the stations to know the time to go to the station.

An universal and simple payment system

One of my favorite this about Taiwan is probably the EasyCard payment system. With a single card you can use in metro, convenient store, supermarkets and YouBike. Probably the best thing is that you can use that card everywhere in Taiwan.

Banks with their debit/credit cards, and phones (via NFC?) have the Easycard payment system integrated. All those following card/debit card integrate the EasyCard payment chip.

A single chip to rule them all.

The right pricing

The Youbike rental system is a pay as you go model. It costs 5 NTD (usd0,17) the first 30 minutes, then 10NTD (usd0.35) per 30 minute. The city also encourages the usage youbike with taking 5NTD on the first 30 minutes on them. To compare the rate with other city in the world:

  • Lyon (1usd/ 45 minute per rental)
  • Paris is 1EUR / 30 minute (~ usd1,20)
  • Germany has a 3euros per 30 minutes rate (~ usd3,50)

Great Bike paths

Taipei city has spent lot of effort building large and safe bike path. It is really pleasant to move around the city.

The riverside bike-path is more than 100 kilometers long! And they are planning to extends those biking paths to expand bicycle trails in the city in the near future.

Major cities have YouBike

An other great thing is that all the biggest cities in Taiwan (Taichung, New Tapei City, Kaoshuing) have YouBike. No matter great or bad, it makes the discovery of a new city frictioneless. You don’t have to subscribe for an other services and worrying about getting back a deposit.

Conclusion

Taipei has made a great job at implementing an amazing infrastructure bike sharing infrastructure. With popen data, incentive to use bikes, maintaining a low price and keeping bikes in a great shape. The steady increases of number of rides in Taipei talks by itself, while other countries see their usage decreasing over years. They’re today transitionning to a second generation of Youbike 2.0, better, lighter, and with docks taking less space.
